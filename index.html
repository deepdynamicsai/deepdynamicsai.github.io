DeepDynamics Lab • IIIT Surat

Deep learning via dynamics.

We build neuroscience-grounded and geometry-aware learning systems for dynamical signals— from chaotic flows to physiological time-series—by blending rigorous theory with practical, reproducible methods.

Our work draws inspiration from cortical organization, thalamocortical gating, and anatomically constrained computation, while leveraging hyperbolic geometry, topological structure, and operator-theoretic perspectives for stable representation learning.

Research philosophy

Modern ML becomes more reliable when it respects the structure of the world: dynamics, geometry, and biology. DeepDynamics emphasizes principled inductive biases—neuro-anatomical constraints, symmetry, hyperbolic structure, and topological memory—to improve robustness, interpretability, and long-horizon behavior.

Focus areas

  • Neuro-inspired reservoirs
    Brain-lateralization, thalamocortical burst gating, event-locked memory mechanisms.
  • Geometry-aware learning
    Hyperbolic representations, equivariant design, and learnable symmetry importance.
  • Dynamics-first time-series
    Chaotic forecasting, operator viewpoints, and robust biomedical prediction.

Research themes

DeepDynamics develops methods that unify biological plausibility, geometric structure, and dynamical stability. Representative directions include:

Neuro-anatomically constrained computation
Reservoirs shaped by cortical asymmetry and thalamocortical gating to enable controllable, event-aligned memory.
Topological and geometric memory
Hodge-anchored and topology-informed state evolution for stable long-horizon forecasting of chaotic flows.
Equivariance and symmetry importance
Learnable symmetry weighting for equivariant networks via adaptive group-weighted convolutions.
Robust forecasting in complex signals
Phase-modulated permutation reservoirs and geometry-aware couplings for physiological and chaotic systems.
